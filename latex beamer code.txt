\begin{frame}{Forces in Motion}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colframe=black,]
Gravity (weight): pulls the jumper downward all the time.\\
\[
F_g=mg
\]
Elastic force (tension): cord pulls upward when stretched.\\
\[
F_R=kx
\]
Air resistance (drag): opposes motion, acting opposite to velocity.\\
\[
F_d=-bv
\]
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{What is the maximum cord stretch and maximum g-force?}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!10,colframe=black,title=Maximum cord stretch]
Maximum cord stretch is the longest extension of the bungee cord during a jump.\\
It occurs at the lowest point when the jumper’s velocity is zero.\\
At this point, the cord stores the most elastic potential energy.\\
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!10,colframe=black,title=Maximum g-force]
Maximum g-force is the largest force per unit weight felt by the jumper.\\
It occurs at the lowest point of the jump when the cord’s tension is greatest.\\
This is when the jumper experiences the strongest upward acceleration.\\
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{How can we calculate maximum cord stretch and maximum g-force?}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!10,colframe=black,title=Maximum cord stretch]
$L$ = total length of the cord\\
$x$ = cord stretch\\
$x_{max}$ = maximum cord stretch\\
$h= L + x$\\
At lowest point all gravitational potential energy becomes elastic potential energy.\\
\[
mgh=\frac{1}{2}kx_{max}^2
\]
\[
mg(L+x)=\frac{1}{2}kx_{max}^2
\]
\[
x_{max}= \sqrt{\frac{2mg(L+x)}{k}}
\]
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=black!10,colframe=black,title=Maximum g-force]
$F_{net}=ma$, $F_{net}=kx_{max}-mg$\\
$ma=kx_{max}-mg$\\
$a=\frac{kx_{max}-mg}{m}$\\
\[
g-force=\frac{a}{g}
\]
\[
g-force=\frac{\frac{kx_{max}-mg}{m}}{g}
\]
\[
g-force=\frac{kx_{max}}{mg}
\]
\end{tcolorbox}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{summary}
\begin{tcolorbox}[colframe=black]
A bungee jump involves a person falling under gravity while attached to an elastic cord.
At first, the jumper is in free fall, experiencing little to no g-force.
As the cord stretches, an elastic force ($F=kx$) pulls upward and slows the fall.
At the lowest point, the cord is maximally stretched and the g-force is greatest.
Afterward, the jumper oscillates up and down until energy is lost due to damping.
\end{tcolorbox}
